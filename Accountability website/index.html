Accountability

Accountability is the quality of a person to own up to their actions and take responsibility for their mistakes. If a team member is lacking accountability, they will lose the trust of their fellow team members and potentially hurt the team more than if they were accountable. Accountability is what allows team member to rely on and work with each other, and without it a team will start to split apart.

Accountability is the glue that holds the teams efforts together!

How to Implement Accountability

One could implement a sort of honor code into the team to increase Accountability. The team could punish people who own up to their mistakes much less than someone who doesn't, or not punish them at all! There could also be incentives for being accountable, like bonuses or raises. It would also help to let employees know exactly what is expeted of them. This way, the employee is less likely to misinterpret requests and orders and will be able to be more Accountable.

How Accountability Helps Business

Accountability is the base of trust in business. If a person cannot be held accountable for their own actions, then that person is not trustworthy. By ensuring that your team and employees are held accountable, you can foster trust among the team as well as among potential business partners. Having accountable employees also creates an environment that can improve work culture. An accountable employee is also more likely to hand in higher-quality work.
